Young Writers Workshops

WATCH VIDEO SIGN UP

Hi, my name is Seth. I mentor children and young adults through insane, funny, dreamy, scary, and beautiful stories that we write together.

Classes are held on Google Meet and based out of Portland, Oregon. Remote learning allows for multiple time zones. Let’s connect from across the world!

If your child needs help with literacy homework, I also am happy to help!

Why a Workshop for Kids?

Collaboration and Connection

Kid-authors create authentic written work that serves the child’s creative spirit while developing school readiness. We compete against the work itself, not for praise or a grade.

Complexity, Depth, and Rigor

Students write shorts stories, plays, screenplays and learn about classical story structure, internal logic, setting and transitions, escalating events, choice and discovery, protagonist vs. antagonist, defining stakes, characterization, theme, genre, point of view, and on and on and on...

Learn to Think Critically

The mechanics of STORY and narrative structure history, belief, and culture. They enrich our inner lives. Children also develop empathy through higher-level thinking.

About Seth

I love what I do. Most recently I taught second grade for seven years at Richmond Elementary, a dual language Japanese immersion program. My approach with students is relational: I flex pedagogy with the child’s nuanced educational and emotional needs within the moment. When not teaching throughout Portland Public Schools or mentoring young writers, I’m busy making stories.
