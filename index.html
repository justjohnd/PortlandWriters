Young Writers Workshops

WATCH VIDEO SIGN UP

Hi, my name is Seth. I mentor children and young adults through insane, funny, dreamy, scary, and beautiful stories that we write together. We start by looking for patterns within texts, then shape a plot that we write together.

Classes are held on Google Meet and based out of Portland, Oregon. Remote learning allows for multiple time zones. Let’s connect from across the world!

If your child needs help with literacy homework, I also am happy to help!

Why a Workshop for Kids?

Collaboration Through Connection

Young-authors create authentic work that serves the child’s creative spirit while developing school readiness. We compete against the work itself, not for praise or a grade.

Complexity + Rigor + Depth

Students learn about classical story structure, internal logic, setting, choice + discovery, protagonist vs. antagonist, defining stakes, characterization, theme, genre, point of view, and so on...

Learn To Think Critically

The mechanics of storytelling structure our histories, beliefs, and cultures. They enrich our inner lives. Children develop empathy through higher-level thinking.

About Seth

I love what I do. Most recently I taught second grade for seven years at Richmond Elementary, a dual language Japanese immersion program. My approach with students is relational. I work to understand a student’s nuanced educational and emotional needs. When not teaching throughout Portland Public Schools or mentoring young writers, I’m busy making stories.

Class Information and Rates

Classes are one hour online. Students need only a notebook and pencil to begin. To serve more families of various income levels, I use a pay-what-you-want sliding scale. The starting-rate begins at $30, with the option to pay more. The average family pays $40. If this cost is still prohibitive, please reach out. I’d like to talk to you.
